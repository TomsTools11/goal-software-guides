# Brand Positioning for Captive Agents

- **Module:** Brand Positioning for Captive Agents
- **Audience:** GOAL Sales Reps and Account Managers
- **Outcome:** Help captive agents compete for price-sensitive shoppers by positioning a local, trust-building brand experience that captures intent first and reveals carrier affiliation at the right moment.

Captive agents face a unique challenge: consumers often associate big carrier brands with “same price everywhere” and default to shopping direct. This module teaches reps how to coach captive agents to:
- Avoid **corporate-brand bias** on top-of-funnel ads and landing pages.
- Build a credible **local identity** that increases form completion.
- Use a **bait → educate → reveal** motion to win trust before price-shopping kills the conversation.

## Learning objectives

By the end, learners can:
- Explain *why* captive branding on ads can reduce conversion and increase bounce.
- Recommend a compliant, practical approach for creating a **DBA / local identity** in creative.
- Coach an agent on landing page positioning that builds curiosity and reduces “price lock-in” assumptions.
- Teach the first-call handoff that preserves trust and frames value before revealing the carrier.
- Handle common objections like “Why does this look different than [Carrier]?” and “I can just get a quote online.”

## Prerequisites

- Understanding of GOAL lead flow and landing page fundamentals.
- Familiarity with consultative discovery basics (agent type, market, strengths, constraints).

## Key terms (use consistently)

- **Captive agent:** Agent primarily selling policies for one carrier.
- **Corporate brand bias:** Consumer assumption they already know the price or can do it directly.
- **Cannibalization:** Corporate DTC campaigns competing with local agents for the same demand.
- **DBA / local identity:** The agency-facing brand used in ads and landing pages.
- **Reveal moment:** When the agent discloses carrier affiliation after trust is established.

## Recommended delivery format

- **Total time:** 45–60 minutes
- **Structure:** 3 lessons + creative workshop + roleplay + knowledge check
- **Assets:** Example ad/LP screenshots, “before/after” positioning examples, call scripts, objection handling prompts

# Lesson 3.1 — The problem with corporate branding on local ads

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Help learners understand the “why” behind the strategy so they can explain it to agents without sounding like they are hiding something.

### Core concepts

- **Consumer bias**
  - When shoppers see a major carrier logo, they assume:
    - “I already know what this costs.”
    - “I can do this online in two minutes.”
    - “This is corporate, not local help.”
- **Cannibalization**
  - Captive agents can accidentally compete with the carrier’s own DTC budget and messaging.

### Sales talk track (coachable)

- “When a shopper sees the corporate logo, it triggers price assumptions. Our goal is to earn a conversation first, then talk carrier.”
- “You are not changing who you are. You are changing how you get a chance to compete.”

### Red flags / pitfalls

- Over-positioning as “independent” in a misleading way.
- Promising outcomes like “you will always beat corporate pricing.”
- Being vague in a way that creates distrust later.

### Learner activity (3–5 minutes)

- **Prompt:** “Why might a shopper bounce when they see a captive brand logo?”
- Collect 3–5 answers and translate them into simple client-facing language.

### Knowledge check

1. What is “corporate brand bias”?
2. What does “cannibalization” mean in this context?

# Lesson 3.2 — The “independent” landing page strategy (DBA / local identity)

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Equip learners with a step-by-step recommendation for how to position the brand at the top of funnel to improve conversion and trust.

### Strategy overview

- Use the agency’s **local identity** (agency name, DBA, local phone, local expertise cues).
- Reduce the prominence of the carrier brand **until after intent is captured**.
- Build curiosity: “local advisor” + “compare options” framing *without misleading claims*.

### What to recommend (practical checklist)

- **Ad and landing page naming**
  - Use “[Agency Name] Insurance Group” or a local DBA.
  - Avoid opening with the parent carrier logo.
- **Trust signals**
  - Local phone number.
  - Local address or service area language.
  - Simple credibility cues (years in business, local service).
- **Curiosity / positioning**
  - Focus on outcome: “help finding coverage that fits your situation.”
  - Use plain language value props that do not imply multiple carriers if that is not true.

### What not to do

- Do not create a brand that implies a different business.
- Do not claim to be “independent” if the agent is captive.
- Do not use bait-and-switch language.

### Workshop (5–7 minutes)

- Provide a “before” example:
  - Headline: “Get a [Carrier] quote now.”
- Create an “after” version:
  - Headline: “Talk to a local agent about coverage options.”
  - Subhead: “Fast quote help from [Agency Name].”
- Learners draft:
  - 1 headline
  - 1 subhead
  - 3 bullets

### Knowledge check

1. What is the goal of using a local identity?
2. What is one trust signal you would add to an LP?

# Lesson 3.3 — The “bait, educate, and reveal” sales motion

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Teach learners how to coach the first call so the agent earns trust first, then transitions smoothly into carrier affiliation.

### The motion (simple stages)

1. **Bait (earn attention)**
   - Local expertise and help, not corporate pricing.
2. **Educate (build trust)**
   - Ask a few discovery questions.
   - Explain how coverage choices affect price and outcomes.
3. **Reveal (disclose carrier at the right time)**
   - Clear, confident disclosure.
   - Reinforce: “You are still getting local help and advocacy.”

### First-call script framework (coach-ready)

- **Opening (local + helpful):**
  - “Thanks for reaching out — I’m a local agent here in [City/State]. I can help you get this sorted quickly.”
- **Discovery (2–4 questions):**
  - “What are you currently insured with?”
  - “What matters most: lowest price, coverage, or service?”
  - “Any recent changes: new car, new drivers, address?”
- **Educate (value framing):**
  - “A small change in deductibles or liability can shift price a lot. I want to make sure you are comparing the right thing.”
- **Reveal (clean transition):**
  - “To be transparent, I write primarily through [Carrier]. The advantage is [service/claims/support]. My job is to make sure you get the right coverage and understand the options.”

### Objection handling roleplay

**Objection 1:** “Why does this not look like [Carrier]?”
- “Great question. You reached a local agency experience first because that’s how we can help you fastest. I am licensed and affiliated with [Carrier], and I’ll make sure you get accurate pricing and coverage guidance.”

**Objection 2:** “I can just get a quote online.”
- “You can. The reason people still use an agent is to avoid missing coverage details that change claims outcomes. If you give me two minutes, I’ll confirm the key details so you’re not surprised later.”

### Learner activity (5 minutes)

- Pair learners.
- Run a 3-minute call simulation with a “reveal moment.”
- Swap roles.

## Module deliverables

Learners should leave with:
- A short explanation of **why** captive branding can hurt conversion.
- A repeatable checklist for **DBA / local identity** positioning.
- A first-call script that includes discovery, education, and a confident reveal.
- 2–3 objection responses that preserve trust.

## Assessment (pass/fail rubric)

Pass if the learner can:
- Explain the strategy without implying deception.
- Recommend a local identity approach with at least 3 trust signals.
- Roleplay the reveal moment clearly and confidently.
- Handle at least one objection without defensiveness.

## Coaching notes for managers

- Coach for clarity and transparency.
- Remove any language that implies misleading “independent” positioning.
- Reinforce the goal: **earn a conversation, then compete on value and service.**

## Appendix — Copy-ready talk tracks

### 30-second pitch (for reps)

“Captive agents often lose at the top of funnel because shoppers see the corporate brand and immediately price-shop or bounce. The fix is to lead with a local agency identity that builds trust and curiosity, capture intent, then handle the first call in a way that educates first and reveals carrier affiliation at the right moment. The result is more conversations and better close rates without trying to win a race to the bottom on price.”

### 10-second definition

“Brand positioning for captive agents is earning trust as a local advisor first, then introducing the carrier once the shopper is engaged.”
