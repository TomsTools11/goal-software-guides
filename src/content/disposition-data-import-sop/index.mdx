# Importing Disposition Data

Disposition data reflects what happened after a GOAL lead was delivered (Contacted, Quoted, Bound). When disposition outcomes are consistently fed back into GOAL, the platform can optimize toward real business outcomes and calculate true **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** and **Cost Per Household (CPH)**.

This SOP covers the full lifecycle — why the feedback loop matters, how to prepare and import data, how to fix errors, and how the data is used for optimization.

---

## 1.0 Strategic Intent: The Disposition Feedback Loop

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Why This Matters">
Without clean and consistent disposition data, optimization is limited and performance reporting can be misleading. Disposition imports act as the optimization "brain" of the GOAL platform.
</TipCallout>

Closing the loop between lead capture and agency outcomes enables:

<InfoCard title="Channel & Traffic Source Clarity" icon="🔍">
Identify high-performing "honey holes" where quoted and bound rates are strongest. Know exactly which sources produce revenue, not just clicks.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Cost Control" icon="💰">
Avoid unproductive bidding wars that inflate costs without returns. Redirect spend to channels that convert.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Real Optimization" icon="📈">
Shift budget based on what converts, not just clicks and leads. Move from CPL-based decisions to CPA-based decisions.
</InfoCard>

## 2.0 Prerequisites and System Access

Before starting a disposition import, confirm the following are in place:

<ChecklistItem id="dispo-prereq-1">**CRM Access** — Ability to export disposition or policy outcome data from the client CRM (Ricochet, Agency Zoom, Lead Manager, etc.)</ChecklistItem>

<ChecklistItem id="dispo-prereq-2">**Quote Burst Typhoon** (when applicable) — If the agency uses Allstate Lead Manager and requires a bridge, confirm Quote Burst Typhoon access is active</ChecklistItem>

<ChecklistItem id="dispo-prereq-3">**Agent ID Mapping** (when applicable) — Confirm the Allstate Agent ID has been provided to GOAL to connect the bridge</ChecklistItem>

<ChecklistItem id="dispo-prereq-4">**Lead Line Configuration** — A dedicated lead line should bypass IVR/phone trees and ring directly to a representative</ChecklistItem>

<ChecklistItem id="dispo-prereq-5">**System Transition Awareness** — If the agency is transitioning systems (e.g., Lightspeed to AAV), confirm the lead line is updated in GOAL immediately</ChecklistItem>

## 3.0 Data Requirements and Structural Standards

### 3.1 Required Identifiers

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Primary Matching Key">
**Phone number is the primary matching key.** Names and physical addresses are not required if the agency prefers a low-PII approach. GOAL matching works best with a stable unique identifier.
</TipCallout>

### 3.2 Required and Optional Outcome Fields

At minimum, capture the outcomes below:

<DataTable
  headers={["Field", "Required?", "Description"]}
  rows={[
    ["Phone Number", "Required", "Primary key used to match GOAL prospects to CRM outcomes"],
    ["contacted", "Required for import", "1 if contact was made. Blank or 0 if not"],
    ["quoted", "Required for import", "1 if a quote was provided. Blank or 0 if not"],
    ["bound", "Required for import", "1 if a sale/policy was issued. Blank or 0 if not"],
    ["Premium", "Optional", "Total premium or policy value. Useful for revenue overlays"],
    ["Household status", "Optional but recommended", "Used to compute Cost Per Household (CPH) and justify total spend"]
  ]}
/>

### 3.3 Status Mapping Standard

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Formatting Rule">
Use **1** for "Yes." Leave **blank** (preferred) or use **0** for "No." Do not use text values like "Yes" or "True" — these will cause import errors.
</TipCallout>

## 4.0 Extracting Disposition Data from Agency Systems

GOAL accepts raw exports from common insurance CRMs and telemarketing systems. Prefer a **raw export** to reduce manual work.

<Accordion title="Ricochet (RICO)">
Primary system of record for LeadMiner outcomes. Export dispositions for both call dispositions and data leads. Make sure the export includes the final disposition status and phone number for matching.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Allstate Lead Manager">
We have an integration set up with Allstate's Lead Manager CRM. The client should send you disposition exports prior to an account review.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="LeadMiner Transfers">
Outcomes often live in Ricochet. Confirm the export includes final disposition status. Cross-reference with GOAL lead data to verify attribution.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Other CRMs (Agency Zoom, etc.)">
Export as CSV or Excel. Ensure phone number is present and disposition columns can be mapped to the contacted/quoted/bound standard. When in doubt, export raw and clean in a spreadsheet before import.
</Accordion>

## 7.0 Submission Workflow and Cadence

Disposition-driven optimization stays current only when reporting is consistent.

<InfoCard title="Recommended Cadence" icon="📅">
**Weekly or bi-weekly** — Best for active accounts with frequent geo/carrier/source changes.

**Monthly** — Minimum standard for ROI tracking and basic performance oversight. Never go longer than monthly.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Common Submission Methods" icon="📤">
**Direct export** — Client emails CSV/Excel export to the assigned Account Manager, who processes the import.

**Prospect View + Bulk Import** — For agencies not using an automated push, the Account Manager pulls data from the GOAL Prospect View and processes the import directly.
</InfoCard>

## 10.0 Tactical Optimization: How the Data Is Used

Disposition overlays allow the GOAL team to move beyond clicks/leads and optimize on profitability.

<InfoCard title="Bid Adjustments" icon="🎯">
**Bid up** on carriers, segments, zip codes, and times of day where quoted and bound rates prove strong. **Bid down** or **exclude** underperforming channels, geographies, or dayparts.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="AI Call Summaries" icon="🤖">
AI-generated call summaries add qualitative context to disposition outcomes, including whether the prospect received a quote, the premium quoted (when available), whether a sale occurred, and clear next steps or coaching opportunities.
</InfoCard>

## 11.0 ROI Analysis and Benchmarks (CPH)

The goal is disciplined spend based on outcomes, not vanity metrics.

<InfoCard title="CPH Benchmarks" icon="📊">
**Ideal target:** $300 CPH

**Competitive benchmarks:** MediaAlpha often $330–$360 CPH, EverQuote around $380 CPH

**Neutral/profitable threshold:** Under $675 CPH
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Why CPH Matters">
Cost Per Household (CPH) is the ultimate performance metric because it accounts for the full value of a customer relationship — not just a single policy. Agencies that track CPH can justify higher lead spend when household bundling is strong.
</TipCallout>

## Critical Reminders

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Manual Entry Does Not Count">
Manual portal entry does **not** push to analytics/reporting. Bulk import is required for data to flow into the optimization engine.

Phone is the best matching key, but watch for landline vs. cell variations and common CRM data quality issues. Normalize phone formats before import.
