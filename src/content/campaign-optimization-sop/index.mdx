## 3.0 Definitions, Success Metrics & Non-Negotiables

### Primary Outcome Metric

<InfoCard title="CPH (Cost Per Household)" icon="🏠">
The primary efficiency target. The goal is to spend only where traffic produces quoteable and sellable households (often multi-policy bundles).

- **Hard benchmark:** CPH under **$675** (neutral-to-profitable threshold)
- **Target goal:** **$300**
</InfoCard>

### Supporting Metrics

<InfoCard title="CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)" icon="💰">
Primary platform KPI used to steer optimization decisions. Measures the total cost to acquire a new customer (a sold policy).
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="CPL (Cost Per Lead)" icon="📊">
Secondary metric — potentially misleading if used in isolation. **Do not chase cheap leads that do not close.** CPL should always be evaluated alongside CPA and CPH.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Data Hygiene Rules">
**The phone number rule:** Use phone numbers as the unique identifier for matching leads to CRM outcomes. Assume names and addresses are noisy and inconsistent. Document every change made in the account and tie changes back to measurable outcomes in the next review.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Strategic Context">

## 6.0 Strategic Campaign Optimization

Optimization is executed across five pillars. Each pillar addresses a different dimension of campaign performance.

### 6.1 Analyzing Campaign Demographics

<Accordion title="Demographic Analysis Procedure">
Demographic analysis enables precise budget allocation toward high-converting segments.

1. **Access data:** Navigate to the Analytics tab within the client's GOAL account.
2. **Review core data points:** Age (highest conversion ranges), Gender (performance by product line), Device type (Mobile vs Desktop quality), Property type (Home campaigns), Number of vehicles (Auto campaigns).
3. **Implementation:** Cross-reference with dispo/CRM outcomes. Deprioritize or exclude underperforming segments. Increase bid allocation for high-performing segments. Prioritize quality over volume to lower CPA and improve CPH.
</Accordion>

### 6.2 Geographic and Zip Code Analysis

<Accordion title="Geographic Optimization Procedure">
Geographic optimization focuses spend on areas with the highest intent and carrier competitiveness.

1. **Data preparation:** Obtain a CRM export containing sales/quote outcomes, zip codes, and phone numbers.
2. **Targeting adjustment:** Map close rates and CPH by zip code using Excel/Google Sheets. Identify **hot zips** (bid up or prioritize) and **black hole zips** (exclude or bid down).
3. **Monitor carrier rate revisions** (often every ~6 months) that can shift geographic performance.
</Accordion>

### 6.3 Source Attribution Analysis

<Accordion title="Source Optimization Procedure">
Source optimization prevents waste by aligning spend to channels that actually produce quotes and sales.

1. **Quality evaluation:** In Analytics, identify Source/Channel/Publisher drivers of lead volume.
2. **Match sources to outcomes:** Use phone-number matching and disposition data.
3. **Optimization action:** Reduce spend on high-volume, low-conversion sources. Protect and scale sources that produce profitable households.
</Accordion>

### 6.4 Establishing Optimization Authority

<Accordion title="Building Strategic Partnership">
Operate as a strategic partner, not a passive reporter.

- **Demonstrate competence:** Present data-backed insights in review meetings and tie changes to CPA/CPH movement.
- **Formalize authority:** Request permission to make adjustments autonomously within agreed parameters.
- **If client prefers control:** Provide a written list of recommended actions after analysis.
- **Documentation:** Log all changes made and report impact at the next review.
</Accordion>

## 7.0 Budget Controls & Dynamic Funding Strategies

Meticulous financial control is required due to the natural ebbs and flows of daily lead volume. A rigid budget is a liability. Strategic flexibility captures high-intent spikes without violating monthly economics.

<InfoCard title="Static / Daily Caps" icon="📅">
Fixed spending limits applied Monday through Friday. Best for agencies with fixed staffing and predictable call capacity.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Dynamic / Monthly Caps" icon="📈">
Run more aggressively day-to-day to capture spikes while adhering to a strict monthly ceiling. Essential for volatile markets where daily caps can block high-intent demand.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Pacing Principle: Front-Load the Week">
Spend should be **front-loaded early in the week** to align with peak staffing. If the team is not available to answer calls or work leads quickly, spend is wasted.
</TipCallout>

## 9.0 Strategic Bid Modifiers & Competitive Conquesting

Bid modifiers enable surgical precision: increase bids on profitable segments without raising universal costs.

<Accordion title="Carrier Specificity (Conquesting)">
Bid up on prospects currently insured by carriers where Allstate is winning on rate. Colorado example: target Farmers customers to exploit the 2% deductible vulnerability.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Demographic Slicing (Honey Holes)">
Combine carrier + age insights. Example: if State Farm ages 25–35 has a high win rate, apply a modifier to own that segment.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Auto-to-Home Feed Strategy">
Auto volume is often **~10x** home volume. Target the auto funnel but filter for homeowners and two-plus cars. This feeds a home-heavy agency while leveraging higher auto inventory.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Day-Parting">
Apply time-based modifiers where quote-to-sale is strongest. Common window: **9:00 AM to 11:00 AM**, but use account data to validate. Bid down during off-hours when staff is unavailable.
</Accordion>

## 12.0 Critical Nuances & Best Practices

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Metric Priority">
Optimize for CPA and CPH. Be cautious using CPL as a decision driver — a low CPL does not guarantee a low CPA.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Phone Number Matching">
Always match via phone number to prevent attribution errors. Names and addresses are too noisy for reliable matching.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Strategic Context Matters">
Apply rules based on the client's niche (preferred vs non-standard, product mix, state constraints). What works in Colorado may not work in Texas.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Carrier & Product Changes">
Rate tables and underwriting shifts can invalidate prior wins. Revalidate assumptions regularly — at minimum every 6 months.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Compliance Readiness">
Keep TrustedForm evidence and logs organized for fast dispute defense. A TCPA complaint can arrive at any time — be ready.
</TipCallout>
