# Module summary

- **Module:** Pitching TCPA Compliance & 1-to-1 Consent
- **Audience:** GOAL Sales Reps and Account Managers
- **Outcome:** Confidently explain the TCPA risk landscape, clarify what “1-to-1 consent” means, and position GOAL’s funnel + proof stack as the safest, highest-integrity way to buy leads.
- This module teaches reps how to lead with *risk reduction* and *proof*, not price. The core narrative is:
  - **The market changed:** Regulators tightened standards and litigators are active.
  - **Most lead sources create exposure:** Shared lead networks and weak consent artifacts are liabilities.
  - **GOAL is different by design:** 1-to-1 consent architecture plus verifiable proof provides a defensible position.

## Learning objectives

By the end, learners can:
- Explain the difference between **shared leads** and **1-to-1 consent** in plain language.
- Identify the common ways agencies get exposed (bad lists, weak consent artifacts, unclear vendor language).
- Walk a prospect through GOAL’s **end-to-end funnel** and why it matters.
- Demonstrate (or describe) GOAL’s **proof stack** (TrustedForm / Jornaya / session replay) as a consent “receipt.”
- Handle the most common objection: **“I can buy cheaper leads elsewhere.”**

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of GOAL platform fundamentals (traffic, campaigns, bidding).
- Familiarity with GOAL’s main lead flow: **click → landing page → form → delivery**.

## Definitions (use consistently)

- **TCPA:** Rules governing calls and texts, consent, and contact practices.
- **1-to-1 consent:** The consumer explicitly requests a quote or contact from one specific business or agent, not a general “network.”
- **Shared lead:** Consumer data is sold or distributed to multiple buyers.
- **Consent artifact:** Documentation showing when and how consent was captured.
- **Proof stack:** The set of tools that provide verifiable evidence of consent (certificate + session replay).

## Recommended delivery format

- **Total time:** 45–60 minutes
- **Structure:** 3 lessons + roleplay + knowledge check
- **Assets:** Slides, demo environment (if available), sample certificates/screenshots, objection handling script

# Session 1.1 — The current regulatory landscape

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Create urgency and relevance without giving legal advice. The learner should feel comfortable saying: “I’m not your attorney, but here is what agencies are facing, and here is why we built GOAL the way we did.”

### Key points to cover

- **Why this matters now**
  - Enforcement and private litigation risk feels higher than ever.
  - Agencies are being targeted because many lead sources cannot prove consent clearly.
- **What “1-to-1 consent” changes**
  - Emphasize that consent is *specific*, not “someone agreed to be contacted by a list of partners.”
- **Shared lead networks are the risk multiplier**
  - Same record sold to many buyers.
  - Consumers receive more calls and are more likely to complain.

### Script framework (talk track)

- “Most agencies do not get sued because they are trying to do something wrong. They get sued because they can’t *prove* the consumer asked for contact.”
- “What GOAL sells is not just leads. It is *documented proof* that the consumer asked for a quote from you.”

### DNC and established business relationship (EBR) (high-level, non-legal)

- Explain at a high level that “do not call” requirements can vary by state and that agencies still need to follow their compliance process.
- Position GOAL as a tool that helps reduce exposure, not eliminate it.

### Learner activity (2–3 minutes)

- **Prompt:** “In one sentence, how would you explain 1-to-1 consent to an agency owner?”
- **Expected output:** Clear, non-technical, and specific.

### Knowledge check (quick)

1. What is the biggest risk with shared lead networks?
2. What does 1-to-1 consent mean in plain language?

# Session 1.2 — GOAL’s exclusive funnel architecture

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Make the architecture easy to visualize so reps can explain “why GOAL is safer” without sounding technical.

### Core story

- GOAL controls the journey from **ad click** to **landing page** to **form submission**.
- Consumer intent is captured **for one agency**.
- Data is not co-mingled with multiple buyers.

### Explain the funnel (simple visual)

- **Step 1:** High-intent click
- **Step 2:** Landing page built for conversion and clarity
- **Step 3:** Consent language that matches 1-to-1 intent
- **Step 4:** Form submit
- **Step 5:** Lead delivery + proof artifact

### Why “self-contained” matters

- Fewer unknowns.
- Clear chain-of-custody for what the consumer saw and did.
- Easier to defend because the interaction is verifiable.

### Differentiation statement (repeatable)

- “We aren’t a lead marketplace or seller like Everquote or QuoteWizard, GOAL is a performance marketing software platform.”

### Learner activity (3–5 minutes)

- **Exercise:** Have learners draw the funnel on paper in 60 seconds.
- **Share:** Each learner gives a 20-second explanation.

### Common pitfalls to avoid

- Over-promising “lawsuit proof.”
- Sounding like legal counsel.
- Talking about tech for tech’s sake.

# Session 1.3 — Leveraging enterprise tech for proof (TrustedForm, Jornaya, session replay)

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes

### Teaching goal

Teach reps to sell *proof*, not just lead volume. A rep should be able to explain what the certificate is, why it matters, and how to use it in the pitch.

### What it is (plain English)

- **TrustedForm / Jornaya:** Creates a time-stamped certificate that documents consent.
- **Session replay:** Shows what the consumer did on the page, which helps resolve disputes.

### Why the buyer should care

- “If someone files a complaint, you need a receipt. This is the receipt.”
- Reduces disputes and buyer anxiety.
- Differentiates from cheaper sources that cannot provide proof.

### Demo flow (if demo is available)

1. Open a sample lead.
2. Locate the certificate.
3. Show timestamp, page, consent language.
4. Show session replay.
5. Tie it back to “one consumer, one agency.”

### Objection handling roleplay

**Objection:** “I buy cheaper leads from [Competitor].”

**Response framework:**
- **Acknowledge:** “Totally fair. Plenty of places sell cheaper leads.”
- **Differentiate:** “The question is: do those leads come with a 1-to-1 consent certificate for every record?”
- **Risk reframe:** “If there’s ever a complaint, what will you show? A spreadsheet or a certificate + replay?”
- **Close:** “If the cheapest lead exposes you to the most risk, is it actually cheaper?”

### Learner activity (5 minutes)

- Pair learners.
- One plays the agent.
- One plays the rep.
- Run the objection and switch roles.
