# Onboarding New Clients

A consistent, repeatable onboarding process that sets expectations, confirms technical setup, and drives a successful launch and early retention — from signup through the 30-day post-launch review.

---

## Purpose & Scope

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Purpose">
Provide a consistent, repeatable onboarding process that sets expectations, confirms technical setup, and drives a successful launch and early retention.
</TipCallout>

This SOP applies to all GOAL Account Managers (and any supporting roles) for **new client onboarding**, from signup through the **30-day post-launch review**.

### Ownership

<InfoCard title="Primary Owner" icon="👤">
**Account Manager (AM)** — owns the entire onboarding process end-to-end.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Client Attendees (Recommended)" icon="👥">
Agency principal, sales manager, and anyone handling inbound calls, CRM, and lead follow-up. Having the right people in the room prevents rework and miscommunication.
</InfoCard>

### Standard Timeline

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Calibration Period">
Typical onboarding is **7–10 business days** from internal setup to launch (varies by agency readiness). The first **30 days** are primarily for calibration and data gathering — set this expectation clearly with the client upfront.
</TipCallout>

## Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding Preparation

Complete these tasks before the onboarding meeting to ensure a focused, efficient session.

### 1. Schedule Onboarding Meeting

<ChecklistItem id="onboard-schedule">Book a 60–90 minute onboarding call with the client</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-stakeholders">Confirm the right stakeholders will attend (principal, sales manager, call handlers)</ChecklistItem>

### 2. Send Confirmation & Agenda

<ChecklistItem id="onboard-calendar">Send a calendar invite with meeting details</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-confirm-email">Send confirmation email covering: meeting duration, what will be finalized, and client prep items (CRM access, lead line ready)</ChecklistItem>

### 3. Pre-Configure Account & Campaigns

<ChecklistItem id="onboard-preconfig">Access the client's GOAL account and pre-configure based on agreement and best practices</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-funnel">Build a baseline Quote Funnel</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-creatives">Draft initial Creatives</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-campaign-template">Set up template campaign structure and baseline targeting</ChecklistItem>

### 4. Initial Client Research

<ChecklistItem id="onboard-research">Review client website, social media, offers, and positioning</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="onboard-recommendations">Prepare initial recommendations for: products offered, geo coverage, lead handling expectations and staffing considerations</ChecklistItem>

### Targeting vs. Modifiers

<InfoCard title="Targeting (Filters)" icon="🎯">
Non-negotiable minimum qualification criteria that **exclude** ineligible prospects:
- Credit score requirements
- Property type exclusions (e.g., mobile homes)
- Carrier exclusions (e.g., competitors)
- Age bands
- Geography
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Modifiers (Bid Adjustments)" icon="🎛️">
Percentage-based bid strategy adjustments based on **value signals**:
- Homeownership status
- Multiple vehicles
- Bundle intent
- Continuous coverage duration

**Example:** 200% bid for Homeowner + 2+ vehicles + open to bundling. 70% bid for Single vehicle + renter.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Lead Delivery — Critical">
Strongly advise a **Dedicated Lead Line** that bypasses IVR / phone trees. For RingCentral, recommend a caller ID prefix (example: "GOAL") for instant lead recognition. About **10%** of leads may call in directly — reduce friction so high-intent prospects reach a salesperson quickly.
</TipCallout>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Follow-Up Is the #1 Success Factor">
A robust follow-up process is the number one predictor of success on the GOAL platform. Set the expectation clearly: **8–10 phone calls, 3–4 automated emails, ~20 touches in 30 days.** Do not re-ask basic qualifying questions if the info is already present in the lead data.
</TipCallout>

## Phase 3: Post-Onboarding Activation

### 1. Send Post-Meeting Summary (Same Day)

<ChecklistItem id="post-summary">Send follow-up email with: key decisions made, client action items, GOAL action items, launch date, and what "good" looks like in week 1</ChecklistItem>

### 2. Finalize Configuration & Launch

<ChecklistItem id="post-finalize">Implement all agreed adjustments from the onboarding meeting</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="post-launch">Activate campaigns on the scheduled launch date</ChecklistItem>

### 3. Initial Performance Monitoring (First 48–72 Hours, Then Week 1–2)

<ChecklistItem id="post-delivery-check">Confirm lead delivery is working end-to-end</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="post-mismatch-check">Monitor for obvious mismatches (wrong geo, wrong product, broken routing)</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="post-early-metrics">Validate early metrics are directionally reasonable</ChecklistItem>

### 4. First 30 Days Focus

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="No Premature Changes">
The first 30 days are for **data gathering** — identify which creatives and profiles perform best. Avoid major optimizations before there is sufficient data. Watch for CRM/lead delivery issues and address them immediately.
</TipCallout>

### 5. 30-Day Review

<ChecklistItem id="post-30day-review">Hold the 30-day performance review</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="post-30day-optimize">Use gathered data to recommend optimizations and refine strategy</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem id="post-30day-cadence">Align on the next cadence (ongoing account review rhythm)</ChecklistItem>

## Critical Success Factors

<InfoCard title="Technical Setup" icon="⚙️">
- Dedicated lead line configured and tested
- CRM integration confirmed and leads flowing
- Caller ID labeling active (if applicable)
- Stakeholders have correct access and credentials
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Client Understanding" icon="🧠">
- Clear timelines and expectations (30+ days for calibration)
- Consent and lead flow expectations understood
- Commitment to robust follow-up process
- Recognition that GOAL requires consistent effort and iteration
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="AM Execution" icon="✅">
- Campaigns pre-configured for meeting efficiency
- Targeting and modifiers aligned to client positioning
- Decisions and next steps documented
- Proactive communication about timeline and expectations
</InfoCard>

## Common Pitfalls

<Accordion title="Pitfall: Inadequate Follow-Up">
**Problem:** Agency calls 1–2 times and stops pursuing the lead.

**Fix:** Set the expectation of 8–10 calls minimum plus an automated email cadence during the onboarding meeting. Reinforce this at every review.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pitfall: Phone Tree Friction">
**Problem:** IVR / phone tree routing reduces contact rates and causes high-intent prospects to hang up.

**Fix:** Insist on a dedicated lead line during onboarding. This is non-negotiable for maximizing ROI.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pitfall: Re-Asking Qualifying Questions">
**Problem:** Staff asks for information the lead already provided (name, address, vehicle info), creating friction and eroding trust.

**Fix:** Ensure staff can access lead details before calling. The data is already there — use it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pitfall: Premature Optimization">
**Problem:** Client wants major campaign changes after only 7–10 days of data.

**Fix:** Educate during onboarding that the first 30 days are for data gathering and calibration. Meaningful optimization requires sufficient volume to identify real patterns.
</Accordion>
