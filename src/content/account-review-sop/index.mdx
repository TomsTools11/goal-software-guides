# Account Review SOP

Standard operating procedure for conducting monthly and quarterly account review meetings with GOAL clients. This process ensures consistent, data-driven account management that strengthens client relationships, optimizes performance, and maximizes retention.

---

## 1.0 Purpose

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Why This SOP Exists">
This document outlines the standard operating procedure for conducting monthly and quarterly account review meetings with GOAL clients (agents). The purpose of this process is to ensure consistent, data-driven account management that strengthens client relationships, optimizes performance, and maximizes client retention.
</TipCallout>

---

## 2.0 Scope

This SOP applies to all GOAL Account Managers responsible for managing client accounts and conducting regular performance reviews.

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Exclusions">
This SOP does not cover initial client onboarding (see the Client Onboarding SOP) or campaign-level optimization procedures (see the Campaign Optimization SOP). It is focused exclusively on the recurring account review cadence.
</TipCallout>

## 3.0 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs are the primary metrics used to evaluate account performance. Account Managers must be proficient in defining, analyzing, and explaining these metrics to clients.

<InfoCard title="CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)" icon="💰">
**Definition:** Total marketing spend divided by the number of policies sold.

**Purpose:** Measures the ultimate ROI of the marketing spend. This is the single most important metric — it tells you whether the money spent on leads is translating into profitable customer acquisition.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="CPL (Cost Per Lead)" icon="📊">
**Definition:** The average cost paid for each individual lead.

**Purpose:** Tracks the efficiency of lead purchasing. While useful as a directional indicator, CPL alone does not reflect whether leads are converting into paying customers.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Contact Rate" icon="📞">
**Definition:** The percentage of total leads successfully contacted by the agency.

**Calculation:** (Leads Contacted / Total Leads) × 100

**Purpose:** Indicates the effectiveness of the agency's lead follow-up process. A low contact rate often signals staffing issues, slow response times, or phone tree friction.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Quote Rate" icon="📝">
**Definition:** The percentage of total leads that have been provided with an insurance quote.

**Calculation:** (Leads Quoted / Total Leads) × 100

**Purpose:** Measures the agency's ability to engage qualified leads and move them through the quoting process.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Close Rate / Win Rate" icon="🏆">
**Definition:** The percentage of quoted leads that result in a bound policy.

**Calculation:** (Policies Bound / Leads Quoted) × 100

**Purpose:** Reflects the sales effectiveness of the agency's producers. This metric reveals whether agents are competitive on rate and effective in their sales approach.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="The Funnel View">
These five KPIs form a sequential funnel: **Spend → Leads (CPL) → Contacted (Contact Rate) → Quoted (Quote Rate) → Sold (Close Rate) → CPA**. A weakness at any stage cascades downstream. Always diagnose the funnel from top to bottom to find the true bottleneck.
</TipCallout>

## 5.0 Escalation Protocol

If an account displays any of the following red flags, the Account Manager must escalate to their direct manager within 24 hours.

<Accordion title="Level 1: Performance Decline">
**Trigger:** A sudden, unexplained performance drop of over 20% in any primary KPI.

**Action:** Notify your manager with a summary of the decline, potential causes identified during analysis, and any immediate corrective actions already taken.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Level 2: Producer or Process Failure">
**Triggers:**
- A producer with a close rate below 10% for over 30 days
- A contact rate consistently below 40%

**Action:** Escalate with producer-level data. Recommend whether the issue is coaching-related (producer skill) or systemic (lead quality, staffing, phone routing).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Level 3: Client Risk">
**Triggers:**
- The client is unresponsive or threatening to cancel
- A technical issue preventing lead flow for more than 48 hours

**Action:** Escalate immediately with full context. If the client is threatening to cancel, include the account history, recent changes, and any known grievances. If technical, include the support ticket reference.
</Accordion>

<TipCallout variant="warning" title="Do Not Delay Escalation">
Waiting beyond 24 hours to escalate a red-flag issue significantly reduces the chance of saving the account. When in doubt, escalate early — your manager would rather hear about a potential issue than discover a lost client.
</TipCallout>

## Key Terminology

Expand each card for a full definition and calculation formula.

<InfoCard title="CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)" icon="💰">
**Definition:** The total marketing spend divided by the number of policies sold. This metric determines the cost to acquire a new customer.

**Calculation:** Total Marketing Spend / Number of Policies Sold
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="CPL (Cost Per Lead)" icon="📊">
**Definition:** The average cost paid for each individual lead.

**Calculation:** Total Spend / Total Leads
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Contact Rate" icon="📞">
**Definition:** The percentage of total leads that the agent or their team has successfully contacted.

**Calculation:** (Leads Contacted / Total Leads) × 100
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Quote Rate" icon="📝">
**Definition:** The percentage of total leads that have been provided with an insurance quote.

**Calculation:** (Leads Quoted / Total Leads) × 100
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Close Rate / Win Rate" icon="🏆">
**Definition:** The percentage of quoted leads that result in a bound policy.

**Calculation:** (Policies Bound / Leads Quoted) × 100
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Answer Rate" icon="📱">
**Definition:** The percentage of inbound calls that are answered by the agency.

**Calculation:** (Inbound Calls Answered / Total Inbound Calls) × 100
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Source" icon="🔗">
**Definition:** A specific traffic channel or category where leads originate, such as Premium Paid Search, Premium Referral, or Social. Sources are the foundation of attribution analysis.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Modifier" icon="🎛️">
**Definition:** A percentage-based bid adjustment applied to specific lead attributes (e.g., gender, device, homeowner status) to optimize for performance.

**Calculation:** Base Bid × Modifier %
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Toolchest / Lead Swami" icon="🔧">
**Definition:** A third-party lead tracking and analytics platform used by some agents to measure performance across all vendors. Useful for benchmarking GOAL performance against other lead sources.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Perfomology" icon="📈">
**Definition:** An Allstate-specific reporting and analytics tool for tracking performance. Often used alongside GOAL's native analytics for a comprehensive view.
</InfoCard>
