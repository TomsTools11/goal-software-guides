## Overview

This training session prepares reps to master the **consultative discovery process** — the foundation of every successful GOAL sales conversation. Reps must demonstrate proficiency across all modules before advancing to platform demonstrations. Performance is measured against the **GOAL Sales Scorecard**.

> **Core Principle:** This framework is not a script to be read verbatim. It is a strategic guide designed to help reps lead with genuine curiosity, understand the prospect's world, and determine if GOAL is the right partner. **A successful discovery earns you the right to present a tailored demo.**
>

## Module 2: Opening & Framing the Call *(First 5 Minutes)*

**Objective:** Reps can confidently open a discovery call, set a two-sided agenda, and surface the prospect's core motivation for the meeting.

**Stage Goal:** Set a professional and collaborative tone, take control of the meeting, and understand the prospect's primary motivation for the conversation.

### 2a. Build Rapport & Set a Two-Sided Agenda

- **Purpose:** Establish yourself as a credible, professional partner who respects the prospect's time.
- **Key Actions:**
    - Start with a brief, professional introduction
    - Acknowledge their time and the context of the meeting
    - Propose a clear, **two-sided agenda** — not a one-sided pitch
- **Example Dialogue:**
    
    > *"Hi [Prospect Name], thanks for taking the time to connect today. I'm looking forward to our chat."*
    > 
    
    > 
    > 
    
    > *"I was hoping we could spend the first few minutes learning a bit more about your agency and what you're looking to accomplish with your marketing. From there, I can walk you through our model and see if it might be a good fit to help you get there. We can leave some time for any questions at the end. Does that sound like a good plan?"*
    > 
- **Training point:** A one-sided agenda signals you're there to sell, not to help. The two-sided agenda builds trust immediately.

### 2b. Uncover the Catalyst — The "Why Now?" Question

- **Purpose:** Understand the specific trigger that led them to this meeting. This is often the **most important question** you will ask on the entire call.
- **The Question (Cold Outbound):**
    
    > *"I know you likely get a lot of calls from marketing companies, so I appreciate you being open to this. To make sure we make the best use of our time, what was it about our outreach that prompted you to want to chat today?"*
    > 
- **The Question (Inbound/Referral):**
    
    > *"What were you hoping to learn about on our call today that made you reach out?"*
    > 
- **Follow-Up Probes — The Curiosity Layer:**
    - *"What does an ideal outcome from this call look like for you?"*
    - *"When you think about your marketing/lead gen right now, what's top of mind?"*
    - *"What are you hoping to find that you're not getting from your current efforts?"*
- **Why timing matters:** The catalyst reveals urgency, pain level, and buying intent. Listen for the real answer beneath the surface response.
- Common catalyst triggers in insurance: losing a key producer, rising acquisition costs, bad experience with a lead vendor
- **Role-play drill:** Catalyst question (cold outbound AND inbound versions) + two follow-up probes, 5 minutes each

## Module 3: Initial Discovery & Strategic Qualification

**Objective:** Reps can efficiently map a prospect's current operation to make a confident go/no-go decision on advancing to a demo.

**Stage Goal:** Get a high-level overview of their operation to quickly determine if there's a fundamental fit. This is the **"go/no-go" stage** for the demo.

### 3a. Understand the Current State

- **Purpose:** Map out their current strategy and business model before going deeper.
- **Key Questions:**
    - *"To get a better sense of your world, could you give me a quick overview of how your agency is currently generating new business?"*
    - *"What's the rough split between your different lead sources, like referrals vs. paid internet leads?"*
    - *"Could you tell me a bit about your team? How many producers are you currently feeding leads to?"*
    - *"How tenured is your team? Are you getting most of your sales from 1-2 people, or is the team pretty consistent?"*
    - *"Are you working with any specific lead vendors right now? Who, if you don't mind me asking?"*
    - *"What does your tech stack look like? What systems are you using?"*
- **Know your CRMs:** **Lead Manager, Ricochet, AgencyZoom** — what each signals about the prospect's sophistication level

### 3b. Reading the Answers

- Heavy referral dependency → opportunity to introduce paid traffic strategically
- No CRM or basic CRM → flag as a potential integration challenge; note as a risk
- Disorganized lead source mix → signals pain around lead quality and cost visibility

### Practice Activity

- Mock discovery call: rep must map a full prospect profile in under 10 minutes
- Debrief: What did you learn? What's still missing? What's the fit signal?

## Module 4: Strategic Disqualification (DQ)

**Objective:** Reps can confidently and professionally end a call when a prospect is not a fit — without burning the relationship or dragging out a dead conversation.

### Why DQ is a Skill, Not a Failure

- Protecting rep time and pipeline quality
- Preserving your credibility — prospects respect reps who don't oversell
- DQ done well often **re-opens doors later**

### Clear DQ Triggers

- Prospect **only wants referrals** and has no interest in a direct-to-consumer digital channel
- Prospect is looking for **cheap data leads** (bulk, low-intent, non-exclusive)
- Budget is fundamentally misaligned with GOAL's model
- No CRM or tracking infrastructure (integration blockers)

### The DQ Framework

1. **Mirror back their model:** *"That's helpful, thank you. So it sounds like your success is built on [repeat their primary channel, e.g., 'building referral relationships']."*
2. **Be transparent about the fit gap:** *"Just to be transparent, our model is focused on generating new opportunities directly from in-market consumers online. Is adding a direct-to-consumer digital channel part of your growth plans?"*
3. **If they confirm they're not a fit:** *"Based on what you've told me, it sounds like you have a great system that's really working for you. Honestly, our model is quite different and might be more disruptive than additive at this stage. To respect your time, it might not make sense for us to continue unless you see a gap I'm missing. Would you agree?"*
4. **End on a high note** — no lingering, no hard sell, leave the door open

### Role-Play Scenarios

- **Scenario A:** Prospect says *"We really just want referrals — we don't buy leads."*
- **Scenario B:** Prospect asks *"Can we get leads for $3 each? That's our budget."*
- **Debrief:** Was the DQ confident? Professional? Did the rep avoid over-explaining or apologizing?

## Module 5: Deep Problem Exploration

**Objective:** Reps can move from "what they do" to "what's broken" — and quantify the financial and operational impact of a prospect's current lead strategy.

**Stage Goal:** Now that fit is established, dig deep into the prospect's pain points. This is where you **build the value** for your solution.

### 5a. Uncover Operational Problems & Challenges

- **Purpose:** Identify the real-world consequences of their current approach.
- **Key Questions:**
    - *"Across all your vendors and campaigns, roughly how much are you spending per month?"*
    - *"When you think about the lead sources you are paying for, what's been working well, and what's been causing the most frustration for you and your team?"*
    - *"Tell me more about that. What does that look like on a day-to-day basis for your producers?"* *(Listen for: burnout, inefficiency, high call volume with low output)*
    - *"When you say the quality is low, what specifically do you mean? Are they bad numbers, are people just not interested when you call?"*
- **Connect operational pain to business risk:** *"How long can you sustain that before it affects retention?"*

### 5b. Quantify the Financial Impact & ROI

- **Purpose:** Attach tangible, financial costs to their pain — essential for building a business case and demonstrating ROI later.
- **Key Questions:**
    - *"That sounds challenging. To help me understand the scale of it, do you have a sense of the numbers behind that? For instance, what are you typically seeing for a cost-per-lead or cost-per-acquisition?"*
    - *"Out of every 100 leads you buy, how many do you typically end up quoting? And of those quotes, how many turn into new business?"*
    - *"What's your target ROI for your marketing spend?"*
- **The Gap Bridge — reflect numbers back:**
    
    > *"So you're spending $X/month, closing at Y%, which puts your CPA around $Z. Does that feel sustainable to you?"*
    > 
- This creates the opening for GOAL's value proposition **without pitching**.

### 5c. Discover Fundamental Business Needs & Long-Term Vision

- **Purpose:** Elevate the conversation from a tactical problem to a **strategic partnership**. This positions you as a forward-thinking consultant, not a vendor.
- **Key Questions:**
    - *"This is all incredibly helpful. Stepping back from the day-to-day for a moment, where do you see your agency's growth coming from in the next 6–18 months? What are the biggest opportunities or threats you're thinking about?"*
    - *"How important is building your own local brand versus just being 'the Allstate/Farmers agent' in your market?"*
    - *"Are you concerned about any shifts in the industry, like changes in rates/underwriting or how consumers shop for insurance?"*

### Practice Activity

- **Financial impact worksheet:** reps calculate prospect CPA from mock data
- **Role-play:** rep must surface AND quantify operational pain in a single 15-minute discovery
- **Debrief:** Did the rep get hard numbers, or accept vague estimates?

## Module 6: Conversational Skills & Call Control

**Objective:** Reps develop the soft skills that keep discovery conversations flowing, productive, and prospect-centered throughout all stages.

### Active Listening

- The difference between **waiting to talk** and **actually listening**
- Techniques: mirroring, labeling, open-ended follow-ups
- Red flag to coach: reps who rush past answers to get to their next question

### Maintaining Dialogue Control

- How to redirect a prospect who goes off-topic without being dismissive
- Anchoring back to the agenda: *"That's really helpful context — I want to make sure we get to [X] before we wrap up. Can I ask you about..."*
- Avoiding the trap of letting the prospect turn the discovery into an informal RFP

### Strategic Pauses

- The power of **silence** after a big question
- How to use pauses to check for comprehension: *"Does that make sense so far?"* / *"How does that land with you?"*
- Pausing after DQ moments — let the prospect sit with the conclusion rather than filling silence with backpedaling

### Skill Drills

- **Drill 1:** 5-minute active listening exercise (rep must summarize prospect's answers verbatim before moving on)
- **Drill 2:** Redirect role-play — prospect goes off-topic, rep must re-anchor professionally
- **Drill 3:** Strategic pause practice — rep asks a big question and holds silence for 10 seconds

## Module 7: Transitioning to the Demo

**Objective:** Reps can bridge discovery and the demo with a tailored transition statement that references the prospect's specific pain — not a generic pivot.

### When You've Earned the Right to Present

A rep earns the right to demo once they have:

- Confirmed strategic fit
- Surfaced 1–2 meaningful pain points
- Attached some financial or operational context to those pain points

### The Transition Statement

> *"Thank you for sharing all of that — it gives me a much clearer picture of your situation. Based on what you've told me about [mention 1–2 key problems, e.g., staff burnout and the need to build a more sustainable marketing channel], I think our model could be a really strong fit. Would it be okay if I shared my screen and walked you through how we would specifically address those challenges?"*
> 
- **Always reference the prospect's own words** — not GOAL's generic features
- The transition is a permission request, not a declaration
- If they say yes, you've set up a **problem-centric demo**, not a feature tour
