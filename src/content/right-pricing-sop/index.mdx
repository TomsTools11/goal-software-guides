# Right Pricing

Standard operating procedure for the Right Pricing of digital advertising campaigns at GOAL. The primary objective is to optimize campaign spending to achieve a target Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), rather than focusing on vanity metrics such as Cost Per Lead (CPL).

---

## Purpose

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Why Right Pricing Matters">
This document outlines the standard operating procedure for the **Right Pricing** of digital advertising campaigns at GOAL. The primary objective is to optimize campaign spending to achieve a target **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**, rather than focusing on vanity metrics such as Cost Per Lead (CPL). Adherence to this SOP ensures a consistent, data-driven approach to bid management across all client accounts.
</TipCallout>

---

## Key Terminology

<InfoCard title="Right Pricing" icon="🎯">
The process of setting and adjusting bids for different traffic sources to achieve a desired cost per acquisition (CPA). It involves analyzing data to determine the optimal price to pay for clicks from various channels.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Base Bid" icon="🏗️">
The initial bid amount set for a campaign or ad group. This is the starting point before any modifiers or adjustments are applied. Think of it as the foundation your entire bidding strategy is built on.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Source Modifiers" icon="🎛️">
Percentage-based adjustments applied to the base bid for specific traffic sources. These are used to bid up or down on channels based on their performance. Example: if a source is 25% more expensive than your base, set its modifier to 125%.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Day Part Schedule" icon="🕐">
A schedule that allows for bid adjustments based on the time of day and day of the week. This is used to optimize bidding during peak performance hours when staff is available to answer calls.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="CPL (Cost Per Lead)" icon="📊">
A vanity metric that measures the cost of acquiring a single lead. While easy to track, it does not reflect the actual cost of acquiring a customer. **Do not optimize for CPL in isolation.**
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)" icon="💰">
The primary metric for success, which measures the total cost to acquire a new customer (a sold policy). All right pricing decisions should be anchored to CPA.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Periscope" icon="🔭">
The internal dashboard used to access the right pricing report and other data for campaign analysis. This is your primary data source for bid decisions.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Traffic Channels / Sources" icon="🔗">
The various platforms and websites where ads are displayed and from which clicks and leads are generated (e.g., Tier 1, Tier 3, Home Search, SEO, Premium Referral).
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="No-Attribution Channel (NA)" icon="❓">
A channel where a user returns to a website directly after an initial visit from a different source, so the click is not attributed to the original source. These represent return visits that are difficult to credit to a specific channel.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Base Bid Best Practice">
Choosing a middle-ground source for the base bid provides a stable foundation. It prevents overpaying on average while still allowing for competitive bidding on higher-quality sources through modifiers. Do not anchor to the cheapest or most expensive source.
</TipCallout>

## Critical Nuances & Best Practices

<Accordion title="Directional Accuracy over Precision">
The goal of right pricing is not to be perfectly precise, but to be **directionally accurate**. The market is dynamic, and prices will fluctuate. Focus on making data-informed adjustments that move you closer to the target CPA rather than chasing exact numbers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Budget-Relative Bidding">
Right pricing is relative to the agent's budget. **Avoid bidding aggressively on high-cost channels for agents with small budgets**, as this can quickly exhaust their funds and lead to poor results. Match bidding ambition to budget capacity.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Data-Driven Decisions Only">
All right pricing decisions must be based on data from the Right Pricing Report and campaign performance — **not on assumptions** or what has worked for other agents in different markets. Every market is different.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="CPA Is the Key Metric">
Always optimize for CPA, not CPL. A low CPL does not guarantee a low CPA, and focusing on CPL can lead to poor quality leads that do not convert. CPA is the only metric that tells you whether you are profitable.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Market Fluctuations">
Be aware of market fluctuations — holidays, major carriers changing their budgets, rate revisions, and regulatory changes can all significantly impact traffic costs. Be prepared to adjust your right pricing strategy accordingly and communicate changes to the client.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The 2x Base Bid Strategy">
An alternative strategy for competitive markets: set the base bid at **2x the target CPC** and then use the day-part schedule to **bid down by 50%** during non-peak hours. This provides a competitive edge during peak times when high-intent traffic is available and staff is ready to answer calls.
</Accordion>
