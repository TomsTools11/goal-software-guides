# Mastering Notion

Welcome to the comprehensive Notion guide for the GOAL Platform team.

## Understanding Notion's Core Philosophy

Notion is a workspace that adapts to you. Unlike traditional tools that lock you into rigid structures, Notion lets you build exactly what you need.

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Key Concept">
Everything in Notion is a page. Pages can hold other pages, creating infinite depth. Think of it like folders that can also contain content.
</TipCallout>

<AnimatedDemo type="notion-sidebar" />

<Accordion title="Pages vs. Traditional Folders">
Unlike a file system where folders only contain files, Notion pages are both containers and content. A page can have text, databases, and child pages all in one place. This eliminates the artificial separation between "where things live" and "what things say."
</Accordion>

### Pages as Building Blocks

Every piece of content in Notion lives on a page. Pages can contain other pages, creating a natural hierarchy that mirrors how your team actually thinks about information.

### The Sidebar

Your sidebar is your navigation hub. Pin important pages, organize by team, and collapse sections you don't need right now.

## The Content Layer: Blocks

Everything in Notion is a block. Text, images, databases, embeds — they're all blocks you can rearrange.

<Accordion title="Text Blocks">
The foundation of any page. Use the slash command `/` to access all block types. Type `/text` for plain text, `/h1` through `/h3` for headings, or just start typing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Media Blocks">
Embed images, videos, files, and web bookmarks. Drag and drop files directly onto a page, or use `/image`, `/video`, or `/file`.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Advanced Blocks">
Callouts draw attention to important information. Toggles hide detail until needed. Synced blocks let you reuse content across multiple pages — edit once, update everywhere.
</Accordion>

### Slash Commands

Press `/` anywhere to see all available block types. Start typing to filter. This is the fastest way to work in Notion.

## Strategic Database Design

Databases are Notion's superpower. They transform simple lists into dynamic, filterable views of your data.

<AnimatedDemo type="notion-database" />

<InfoCard title="Text & Number Properties" icon="📝">
Text for names and descriptions. Numbers for quantities, scores, and budgets. Use number formats (currency, percent) to keep data consistent.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Select & Multi-Select" icon="🏷️">
Select for single-choice fields like Status (To Do, In Progress, Done). Multi-select for tags and categories where items can have multiple labels.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Date & People" icon="📅">
Date properties support ranges and reminders. People properties assign owners and collaborators, making it clear who's responsible for what.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Relation & Rollup" icon="🔗">
Relations connect databases together (e.g., Tasks → Projects). Rollups aggregate data across relations (e.g., count tasks per project, sum budget items).
</InfoCard>

### Linked Databases

Reference the same data in multiple places. Filter differently per view without duplicating information.

## Views, Filters, and Templates

Transform how you see your data with views. Each view can have its own filters and sorts.

<Accordion title="Table View">
The default view. Great for data entry and seeing all properties at once. Sort by any column, filter by any property.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Board View">
Kanban-style columns. Group by a select property (like Status) and drag items between columns. Perfect for project tracking.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Calendar View">
See items on a timeline based on their date property. Great for content calendars, sprint planning, and deadline tracking.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gallery View">
Card-based view that emphasizes visual content. Ideal for design assets, portfolios, and content libraries.
</Accordion>

<TipCallout variant="tip" title="Dashboard Tip">
Create a dashboard page with multiple linked databases, each showing a different filtered view. This gives you a command center for your entire workflow.
</TipCallout>

## Notion Calendar and Mail

Integrate your calendar and email workflow directly into Notion.

<TipCallout variant="important" title="The TARA System">
**T**asks, **A**genda, **R**eferences, **A**ction items — use this framework to structure every meeting note. Create a template with these four sections and apply it before every meeting.
</TipCallout>

<InfoCard title="Calendar Integration" icon="📆">
Connect Google Calendar or Outlook to see events alongside your Notion tasks. Create Notion pages directly from calendar events for seamless meeting notes.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Notion Mail" icon="✉️">
Forward emails into Notion to capture action items. Use the email-to-page feature to turn client correspondence into trackable tasks.
</InfoCard>
