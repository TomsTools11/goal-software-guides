## Understanding Notion's Core Philosophy

Notion is a workspace that adapts to you. Unlike traditional tools that lock you into rigid structures, Notion lets you build exactly what you need.

### Pages as Building Blocks

Every piece of content in Notion lives on a page. Pages can contain other pages, creating a natural hierarchy.

### The Sidebar

Your sidebar is your navigation hub. Pin important pages, organize by team, and collapse sections you don't need right now.

## Crafting the Professional Page

A well-structured page communicates professionalism and makes information easy to find.

### Icons and Typography

Choose meaningful icons for your pages. Use headers consistently to create scannable content.

### Page Width

Full-width pages work great for databases and dashboards. Standard width keeps text readable.

## The Content Layer: Blocks

Everything in Notion is a block. Text, images, databases, embeds — they're all blocks you can rearrange.

### Text Blocks

The foundation of any page. Use the slash command `/` to access all block types.

### Advanced Blocks

Callouts, toggles, and synced blocks help you organize complex information.

## Strategic Database Design

Databases are Notion's superpower. They transform simple lists into dynamic, filterable views of your data.

### Property Types

Text, numbers, dates, selects, relations — each property type serves a specific purpose.

### Linked Databases

Reference the same data in multiple places. Filter differently per view without duplicating information.

## Views, Filters, and Templates

Transform how you see your data with views. Each view can have its own filters and sorts.

## Buttons and Automations

Automate repetitive tasks with Notion's button and automation features.

Integrate your calendar and email workflow directly into Notion.
