# Mastering the Platform Demo

This module bridges the discovery phase into a tailored, compelling platform demonstration. By the end of this session, reps will be able to confidently walk a prospect through the GOAL platform, connect each feature to the prospect's specific pain points uncovered in discovery, and handle common objections in real time.

**Prerequisites:** Completion of Module 2 (Consultative Discovery Process)

**Audience:** New sales reps, account managers in onboarding

**Estimated Duration:** 90 minutes (60 min instruction + 30 min live practice)

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, reps will be able to:
- [ ] Walk a prospect through the full QuoteFunnel consumer experience and articulate its brand-building value
- [ ] Confidently deliver the "1-in-10" call hook as a natural part of the demo flow
- [ ] Navigate the GOAL backend analytics to demonstrate source-level transparency
- [ ] Pull up and explain AI Conversations call analytics using a live account
- [ ] Explain TCPA compliance and TrustedForm protection in plain language a prospect can understand

## Section 1: Setting Up the Demo

### 1.1 Bridging Discovery to Demo

> *"Everything you show in the demo should connect back to something the prospect told you."*

- **Key principle:** The demo is not a feature tour — it's a proof-of-concept conversation
- Reference 2–3 specific pain points from discovery before launching the screen share
- Use a transition phrase:
  - *"Based on what you told me about [pain point], let me show you exactly how GOAL addresses that..."*
- Have a live account open and ready before the call — never navigate live without preparation

### 1.2 Demo Environment Checklist

- [ ] Live account pulled up in GOAL Admin Portal
- [ ] QuoteFunnel preview link ready for the prospect's vertical/state
- [ ] Backend analytics dashboard loaded with recent data
- [ ] AI Conversations tab open with at least 3–5 recent call transcripts
- [ ] Screen share tested and working

## Section 2: The QuoteFunnel & The "Final Destination"

### 2.1 What to Show

- Navigate to a live QuoteFunnel branded to the agency
- Walk through the consumer experience step-by-step:
  1. Consumer lands on the funnel page
  2. They complete the quote form
  3. They reach the **final confirmation page**

### 2.2 Key Talking Points

**Branding & Local Equity**
- The funnel is fully branded with the **agency's name, logo, and local identity**
- Consumers associate the experience with the agency — not a third-party lead vendor
- *"This is building your brand every time someone interacts with your funnel, even if they don't convert immediately."*

**The "Final Destination" Concept**
- The final page has **zero competing carrier ads**
- There is **one and only one call to action**: call the agency directly
- Contrast this explicitly with aggregator experiences where consumers are shown 5–10 competing agents
- *"Your prospect isn't being handed off to your competitors. This is their final destination — and the only next step is calling you."*

### 2.3 Objection Handling

## Section 3: The "1-in-10" Call Hook

### 3.1 The Statistic

- On average, **1 out of every 10 leads** generated will click the inbound call button on the QuoteFunnel
- These are **high-intent warm transfers** — the consumer initiated the call, not the agent

### 3.2 How to Deliver It

- Frame it in the prospect's own numbers:
  - *"If GOAL generates 100 leads for your agency this month, you can expect roughly 10 of those to call you directly — inbound, warm, and ready to quote."*
- Connect it to cost-per-acquisition:
  - *"Those inbound calls typically close at a significantly higher rate than outbound — so they're often your highest-ROI leads in the bunch."*
- Let the math land before moving on — pause after stating the ratio

### 3.3 Practice Exercise

> Reps pair up. One plays the prospect, one delivers the 1-in-10 hook using the prospect's hypothetical lead volume. Switch and debrief.

## Section 4: Backend Transparency & Source Analytics

### 4.1 Why This Matters

- Many prospects have been burned by "black box" lead vendors who can't explain where leads come from
- Showing the backend directly addresses the trust gap
- **Goal:** Prove that GOAL is fully accountable and auditable

### 4.2 What to Show in the Backend

Navigate to the analytics dashboard and walk through:
1. **Click-level tracking** — Show that every click is logged with source attribution
2. **Lead-level data** — Each lead is tied to the exact traffic source that generated it
3. **CPA by source** — Demonstrate how cost-per-acquisition varies by source and how GOAL uses this to optimize
4. **Source filtering** — Show how underperforming sources can be paused or excluded

### 4.3 Key Talking Points

- *"You can see every dollar you spend and exactly where it went — down to the traffic source."*
- *"If a source isn't performing, we don't just tell you — we show you the data and we turn it off."*
- Emphasize that this level of transparency is **rare in the lead gen industry**

### 4.4 Common Questions to Anticipate

- *"What sources does GOAL use?"* → Traffic partners, search, display — explain at a high level and offer a deeper dive post-demo
- *"Can I block certain sources?"* → Yes, source-level controls are available

## Section 5: AI "Conversations" Call Analytics

### 5.1 Setup

- Pull up the **AI Conversations** tab in a live account
- Select 2–3 recent call transcripts that show clear AI summaries
- Ideally, pre-select one with a sale and one without, to show the range of insights

### 5.2 The Three Questions the AI Answers

For every recorded call, the AI automatically evaluates:
1. ✅ **Was a quote provided?**
2. ✅ **Did a sale occur?**
3. ✅ **What could the agent have done better?**

### 5.3 Key Talking Points

- *"This is like having a sales coach reviewing every single call your agency takes — automatically."*
- For agency owners with multiple producers: *"You can monitor your entire team's call quality without listening to every call yourself."*
- Frame the coaching question (#3) as a growth tool, not a surveillance tool

### 5.4 Demo Flow

1. Open a call transcript
2. Read the AI summary aloud (or let the prospect read it)
3. Point out the three automated answers
4. Ask the prospect: *"How are you currently reviewing call quality on your team?"* → Let their answer create the contrast

### 5.5 Practice Exercise

> Reps pull up a sample transcript and practice narrating the AI Conversations feature as if presenting to a new prospect. Trainer gives feedback on clarity and pacing.

## Section 6: TCPA Compliance

### 6.1 Why This Is a Demo Must-Have

- TCPA litigation is a **real and growing concern** for insurance agencies
- Prospects will often raise it — reps must get ahead of it confidently
- Positioning GOAL's compliance tools as **protection** reframes cost as risk management

### 6.2 What to Explain

**1-to-1 Documented Consent**
- Every lead generated through GOAL's QuoteFunnel includes **1-to-1 documented consent**
- The consumer specifically consented to be contacted by **this agency** — not a shared consent form
- This is the gold standard for TCPA compliance

**TrustedForm Session Replays**
- GOAL integrates with **TrustedForm** to capture a full session replay of each consumer completing the form
- The replay is timestamped and stored as legal evidence
- *"If you ever face a TCPA claim, you have a video of the consumer filling out the form and checking the consent box. That's your defense."*

### 6.3 Key Talking Points

- *"You're not buying leads from a pool of consumers who checked a generic 'I agree' box. You're getting leads who consented to hear from your agency specifically."*
- *"TrustedForm doesn't just protect you — it's documentation that plaintiffs' attorneys know is near-impossible to challenge."*

### 6.4 What NOT to Do

> ⚠️ **Do not provide legal advice.** If a prospect has specific legal questions about their TCPA exposure, direct them to their legal counsel. Reps can explain what the platform does — not what it legally guarantees.

## Section 7: Bringing It Together — The Full Demo Flow

### 7.1 Recommended Demo Sequence

1. **Open** with discovery recap (2 min)
2. **QuoteFunnel walkthrough** — branded experience + Final Destination (5–7 min)
3. **1-in-10 call hook** — deliver the stat, run the math with their numbers (3 min)
4. **Backend analytics** — source-level transparency (5–7 min)
5. **AI Conversations** — pull a live transcript, walk through the three questions (5 min)
6. **TCPA compliance** — 1-to-1 consent + TrustedForm (3–4 min)
7. **Close** with a question: *"Based on what you've seen, what resonates most?"* (open-ended, not a hard close)

### 7.2 Timing Guidance

- Total demo target: **25–30 minutes**
- If a prospect engages heavily on one section, let them — engagement is a buying signal
- Do not rush through compliance — it's often a silent concern even if not voiced

## Section 8: Practice & Role-Play

### 8.1 Full Demo Role-Play

- Trainer plays a skeptical prospect (e.g., previously burned by a lead vendor)
- Rep delivers the full demo sequence from Section 7.1
- Debrief focuses on:
  - Confidence and pacing
  - How well the demo connected to "discovery" findings
  - Handling of the TCPA section
  - Natural delivery of the 1-in-10 hook

### 8.2 Objection Gauntlet

Trainer fires rapid objections. Rep responds without stopping the demo flow:
- *"I've tried lead vendors before and they never work."*
- *"How is this different from EverQuote or MediaAlpha?"*
- *"What if I get sued for calling a lead?"*
- *"This seems expensive."*
- *"I need to think about it."*

### 8.3 Self-Assessment Checklist

After each practice run, reps self-assess:
- [ ] I connected each demo section to a discovery finding
- [ ] I delivered the 1-in-10 stat with confidence and ran the math
- [ ] I showed backend analytics without getting lost in the data
- [ ] I pulled up AI Conversations and narrated the three questions clearly
- [ ] I explained TCPA compliance without giving legal advice
- [ ] I ended with an open-ended question, not a close
