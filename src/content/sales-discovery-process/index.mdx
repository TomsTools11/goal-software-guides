# GOAL Sales Discovery Process

Master the consultative discovery process — the foundation of every successful GOAL sales conversation.

---

## Overview & Core Principle

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Purpose">
This training prepares reps to master the consultative discovery process. Reps must demonstrate proficiency across all modules before advancing to platform demonstrations. Performance is measured against the GOAL Sales Scorecard.
</TipCallout>

<InfoCard title="Core Principle" icon="🧭">
This framework is not a script to be read verbatim. It is a strategic guide designed to help reps lead with genuine curiosity, understand the prospect's world, and determine if GOAL is the right partner. **A successful discovery earns you the right to present a tailored demo.**
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="What Success Looks Like" icon="🎯">
By the end of this training, you will be able to confidently open a discovery call, qualify or disqualify a prospect, quantify their pain, and earn the right to present a tailored demo — all measured against the GOAL Sales Scorecard.
</InfoCard>

## Module 1: Sales Scorecard & Evaluation Framework

<InfoCard title="Phase 1: Discovery Quality" icon="🔍">
Were the right questions asked? Did the rep uncover real pain? Did they get hard numbers? Discovery quality is the foundation — without it, the demo has no anchor.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Phase 2: Call Control & Skill" icon="🎙️">
Did the rep manage time, listen actively, and use strategic pauses? Conversational skill separates order-takers from consultants.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Phase 3: Demo Execution" icon="💻">
Did the demo connect directly to the prospect's problems? Was the value prop tailored, not generic? Did the rep differentiate GOAL from lead vendors?
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Phase 4: Close & Commitment" icon="🤝">
Did the rep set expectations, handle objections with data, and gain a clear next step? Closing is the natural result of a great discovery.
</InfoCard>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Key Message">
The scorecard is a coaching tool, not a gotcha — it protects reps and clients alike. Mastering discovery is a prerequisite to showing the platform.

## Module 2: Opening & Framing the Call

**Objective:** Confidently open a discovery call, set a two-sided agenda, and surface the prospect's core motivation for the meeting.

### Build Rapport & Set a Two-Sided Agenda

A one-sided agenda signals you're there to sell, not to help. The two-sided agenda builds trust immediately.

### Uncover the Catalyst — The "Why Now?" Question

This is often the **most important question** you will ask on the entire call. The catalyst reveals urgency, pain level, and buying intent.

<Accordion title="Catalyst Triggers to Listen For">
- **Losing a key producer** — signals staffing instability and urgency to find new lead sources
- **Rising acquisition costs** — the prospect is already feeling the squeeze of traditional lead vendors
- **Bad experience with a lead vendor** — frustration that opens the door to GOAL's differentiated model
- **Growth mandate from ownership** — top-down pressure means they need results fast
- **Market shift (rates, regulations)** — external forces creating urgency to act now
</Accordion>

## Module 4: Strategic Disqualification

**Objective:** Confidently and professionally end a call when a prospect is not a fit — without burning the relationship.

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Why DQ is a Skill, Not a Failure">
Protecting rep time and pipeline quality. Preserving your credibility — prospects respect reps who don't oversell. DQ done well often re-opens doors later.
</TipCallout>

<StepByStep steps={[
  {
    title: "Step 1: Mirror Back Their Model",
    content: "\"That's helpful, thank you. So it sounds like your success is built on [repeat their primary channel, e.g., 'building referral relationships'].\""
  },
  {
    title: "Step 2: Be Transparent About the Fit Gap",
    content: "\"Just to be transparent, our model is focused on generating new opportunities directly from in-market consumers online. Is adding a direct-to-consumer digital channel part of your growth plans?\""
  },
  {
    title: "Step 3: Confirm the Misfit (If They Agree)",
    content: "\"Based on what you've told me, it sounds like you have a great system that's really working for you. Honestly, our model is quite different and might be more disruptive than additive at this stage. To respect your time, it might not make sense for us to continue unless you see a gap I'm missing. Would you agree?\""
  },
  {
    title: "Step 4: End on a High Note",
    content: "No lingering, no hard sell, leave the door open. Thank them for their time, wish them continued success, and let them know you're available if their needs change."
  }
]} />

**Prospect says:** *"We really just want referrals — we don't buy leads."*

**Your approach:** Mirror back their model, acknowledge it's working, and be transparent that GOAL is a direct-to-consumer digital channel. If they're not interested in that channel, DQ professionally. Don't try to convince someone who doesn't want digital leads.
</Accordion>

</Accordion>

## Module 5: Deep Problem Exploration

**Objective:** Move from "what they do" to "what's broken" — and quantify the financial and operational impact.

### Operational, Financial & Strategic Discovery

<InfoCard title="Operational Problems" icon="⚙️">
Surface the real-world consequences of their current approach. Listen for: burnout, inefficiency, high call volume with low output, staff frustration, and wasted time chasing bad leads.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Financial Impact" icon="💰">
Attach tangible financial costs to their pain. Get real numbers: monthly spend, cost-per-lead, cost-per-acquisition, quote-to-close ratio, and target ROI. These numbers anchor the value conversation later.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Strategic Vision" icon="🔭">
Elevate from tactical problems to strategic partnership. Understand their 6–18 month growth plans, brand ambitions, and concerns about industry shifts. This positions you as a consultant, not a vendor.
</InfoCard>

## Module 6: Conversational Skills & Call Control

**Objective:** Develop the soft skills that keep discovery conversations flowing, productive, and prospect-centered.

<InfoCard title="Active Listening" icon="👂">
The difference between **waiting to talk** and **actually listening**. Use mirroring, labeling, and open-ended follow-ups. Red flag: reps who rush past answers to get to their next question.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Dialogue Control" icon="🎯">
Redirect without being dismissive: *"That's really helpful context — I want to make sure we get to [X] before we wrap up. Can I ask you about..."* Avoid letting the prospect turn discovery into an informal RFP.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Strategic Pauses" icon="⏸️">
The power of silence after a big question. Use pauses to check for comprehension: *"Does that make sense so far?"* / *"How does that land with you?"* Let the prospect sit with conclusions rather than filling silence.
</InfoCard>

## Module 7: Transitioning to the Demo

**Objective:** Bridge discovery and the demo with a tailored transition statement that references the prospect's specific pain.

### When You've Earned the Right to Present

A rep earns the right to demo once they have:
- Confirmed strategic fit
- Surfaced 1–2 meaningful pain points
- Attached some financial or operational context to those pain points

<CopyableTemplate
  title="Demo Transition Statement"
  sections={[
    {
      heading: "Transition Script",
      fields: [
        { label: "Transition:", placeholder: "Thank you for sharing all of that — it gives me a much clearer picture of your situation. Based on what you've told me about [mention 1–2 key problems, e.g., staff burnout and the need to build a more sustainable marketing channel], I think our model could be a really strong fit. Would it be okay if I shared my screen and walked you through how we would specifically address those challenges?" }
      ]
    }
  ]}
/>

<TipCallout variant="important" title="Key Principle">
Always reference the prospect's own words — not GOAL's generic features. The transition is a permission request, not a declaration. If they say yes, you've set up a problem-centric demo, not a feature tour.
</TipCallout>

## Practice Scenarios & Role-Play Reference

Use these scenarios for practice sessions and self-evaluation.

<Accordion title="Scenario 1: The Referral-Dependent Agency">
**Setup:** A 10-person agency that generates 90% of business from referrals. The owner heard about GOAL from a friend and is "curious but not committed."

**Discovery goals:**
- Uncover whether they have any interest in a digital channel
- Understand if their referral pipeline is sustainable
- Determine if they have the infrastructure (CRM, lead line) to support GOAL
- Practice the DQ framework if they're not a fit

**Success criteria:** Rep either (a) surfaces a real gap that GOAL can fill and earns the right to demo, or (b) professionally disqualifies and leaves the door open.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Scenario 2: The Frustrated Lead Buyer">
**Setup:** A mid-size agency spending $5,000/month on leads from EverQuote and SmartFinancial. The owner is frustrated with low contact rates and "garbage leads" but doesn't know there's an alternative model.

**Discovery goals:**
- Quantify their current spend, CPA, and conversion rates
- Surface the emotional and operational pain of shared leads
- Use the Gap Bridge to reflect their numbers back
- Transition to a problem-centric demo

**Success criteria:** Rep gets hard financial numbers, quantifies the pain, and delivers a tailored transition statement.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Scenario 3: The Tech-Savvy Growth Agency">
**Setup:** A growth-oriented agency with a dedicated marketing person. They use AgencyZoom, have a lead line, and are evaluating multiple vendors. They want to see GOAL's platform and compare.

**Discovery goals:**
- Map their current vendor mix and evaluation criteria
- Understand what "success" looks like for them (CPA target, volume goals)
- Position GOAL as the only option that gives them ownership, not just access
- Avoid letting the call become an informal RFP

**Success criteria:** Rep maintains control of the conversation, differentiates GOAL from vendor comparisons, and secures a focused demo.
</Accordion>
