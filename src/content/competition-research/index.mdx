- **5x Conversion:** Because leads are exclusive and brand-aligned, GOAL leads convert at up to 5x the rate of shared marketplace leads.
</Accordion>

## Competitive Battlecards

Use these battlecards during live sales conversations. Each contains the competitor's pitch, GOAL differentiators, objection handling, and landmine questions.

<Accordion title="Battlecard: EverQuote">
**Quick Overview:** Largest online insurance lead marketplace. Sells shared leads and live call transfers. Target: all agents looking for volume. Pay-per-lead pricing.

**Their Pitch:** "#1 online insurance marketplace with 2M+ monthly shoppers and 15+ years of experience."

**GOAL Differentiators:**
- Every lead is exclusively yours vs. shared with multiple agents
- Your agency brand on the QuoteFunnel vs. invisible brand
- Pay-per-click with transparent pricing vs. pay-per-lead with less visibility
- 5x conversion rate due to exclusivity and brand alignment

**Objection Handling:**

| If They Say... | Respond With... |
|---|---|
| "EverQuote has more experience" | They do have a long track record — built on the old model of shared leads. We're built for where the industry is going: agents owning their customer relationships from the first click. |
| "EverQuote has 2M shoppers" | Impressive volume. But if those shoppers are sold to 4–8 agents each, how many can YOU convert? With GOAL, every shopper who clicks your funnel is yours alone. |
| "I'm already using EverQuote" | Many of our agents started on EverQuote. They found that even with high volume, conversion was low because they were racing other agents. They use GOAL to build a predictable pipeline alongside other sources. |

**Landmines to Set:**
- "When a consumer fills out a form on EverQuote, how many agents get that same lead?"
- "When those consumers get calls from 4+ agents, how does that affect your close rate?"
- "How important is it for your brand to be the first thing a consumer sees?"

**Win/Loss:** We win when the agent values quality over quantity, wants their own brand, or is frustrated with low contact rates. We lose when the agent prioritizes massive volume above all else or prefers full-service "do it for me."
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Battlecard: MediaAlpha">
**Quick Overview:** Programmatic ad exchange for insurance. Real-time bidding marketplace. Target: large carriers and tech-savvy agency groups. Auction-based pricing. $45M FTC settlement in 2025.

**Their Pitch:** "The leading programmatic platform for insurance customer acquisition."

**GOAL Differentiators:**
- SOC2 + TCPA compliant vs. $45M FTC settlement for deceptive practices
- Purpose-built for independent agents vs. built for large carriers with media teams
- Simple dashboard with branded funnels vs. complex programmatic bidding platform

**Objection Handling:**

| If They Say... | Respond With... |
|---|---|
| "MediaAlpha has more technology" | Sophisticated tech built for media buying teams at large carriers. Our technology is purpose-built for agents — simple to use and focused on exclusive leads at controllable cost. |
| "MediaAlpha has more scale" | They do move huge volume. But ask how much is compliant. They just paid $45M to the FTC. With GOAL, every lead is generated through your branded funnel with full SOC2 and TCPA compliance. |

**Landmines to Set:**
- "How important is regulatory compliance to your agency? Are you aware of any recent FTC actions in this space?"
- "Do you have a dedicated media buying team, or do you need something an agent can use directly?"
- "When you buy leads from an exchange, do you know how those leads were actually generated?"

**Win/Loss:** We win when the agent values compliance and brand safety, doesn't have a media team, or is aware of the FTC settlement. We lose when the agent is a large carrier with a media buying team.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Battlecard: NextGen Leads">
**Quick Overview:** Self-serve insurance lead bidding platform. Second price auction. No minimums. #1 in Under-65 Health. Target: cost-conscious agents who want pricing control.

**Their Pitch:** "Performance-driven insurance leads with full transparency."

**GOAL Differentiators:**
- Control over everything (pricing, branding, exclusivity) vs. just pricing control
- Building your own lead engine vs. bidding for shared leads
- Full-line coverage vs. primarily health/Medicare/auto

**Objection Handling:**

| If They Say... | Respond With... |
|---|---|
| "NextGen has a fair auction model" | Their second price auction is smart for shared leads. But the fairest model is one where the lead is exclusively yours. No auction needed when you're the only bidder. |
| "NextGen has no minimums" | GOAL also gives you full budget control. The difference is what you get — exclusive leads through your branded funnel vs. shared leads through a generic form. |

**Landmines to Set:**
- "When you win a lead at auction on NextGen, how many other agents also got that lead?"
- "Do you sell health insurance only, or do you want leads across multiple lines?"
- "Would you rather bid on shared leads or invest in building your own pipeline?"

**Win/Loss:** We win when the agent wants exclusive leads (not just cheaper shared ones), sells multiple lines, or wants brand presence. We lose when the agent only sells health and is highly price-sensitive.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Battlecard: QuoteWizard (LendingTree)">
**Quick Overview:** High-volume insurance lead marketplace backed by LendingTree. Shared leads capped at 4 agents. 300,000+ leads/month. 7,000+ agents. 25% return policy.

**Their Pitch:** "Insurance leads that work, backed by LendingTree."

**GOAL Differentiators:**
- 1 agent per lead vs. 4 agents competing
- Quality built in (branded funnel) vs. 25% return policy (1 in 4 expected bad)
- Your brand as hero vs. LendingTree brand

**Objection Handling:**

| If They Say... | Respond With... |
|---|---|
| "QuoteWizard only shares with 4 agents" | Four is better than eight, but it's still four agents calling the same person. With GOAL, you're the only one. That's why our agents see dramatically higher contact and close rates. |
| "They have a return policy" | A 25% return policy means they expect a quarter of leads to be bad. We'd rather give you leads that don't need returning because they came through your own branded funnel. |
| "LendingTree is a known brand" | Known for loans, not insurance. Your agency brand is what matters to your local market. GOAL puts your brand front and center. |

**Landmines to Set:**
- "When 4 agents all call the same consumer, what's your typical contact rate?"
- "How often do you use that 25% return policy? What does that say about baseline quality?"
- "Would your consumers trust your agency brand more or the LendingTree brand?"

**Win/Loss:** We win when the agent is frustrated racing other agents, values brand building, or cares about lead quality. We lose when the agent wants maximum volume at lowest per-lead cost or trusts LendingTree implicitly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Battlecard: SmartFinancial">
**Quick Overview:** Digital insurance marketplace offering shared leads, exclusive leads, and live transfers via Insurance.io. Data leads: 15–18% close rate. Live transfers: 22–23%. Target: agents wanting flexibility.

**Their Pitch:** "Multiple ways to grow your insurance business."

**GOAL Differentiators:**
- Exclusivity is the default, not a premium upsell
- Lower CPA through branded funnel vs. premium pricing for exclusive leads
- Your brand on every touchpoint vs. marketplace branding

**Objection Handling:**

| If They Say... | Respond With... |
|---|---|
| "SmartFinancial offers exclusive leads too" | They do — at a premium price. With GOAL, exclusivity isn't an upsell. It's how every lead works. And because they come through your branded funnel, the CPA is lower. |
| "Their live transfers close at 22%" | Live transfers are great but expensive. What if you could get close rates approaching that with data leads because they're exclusive and brand-aligned? That's what GOAL delivers. |

**Landmines to Set:**
- "What percentage of your SmartFinancial leads are shared vs. exclusive?"
- "How much more do you pay for their exclusive product vs. shared?"
- "Do consumers know your agency name when you call, or do they think it's just another insurance call?"

**Win/Loss:** We win when the agent wants exclusivity without paying a premium, wants their brand visible, or is focused on cost-per-acquisition. We lose when the agent loves live transfers and is willing to pay for them.
</Accordion>
