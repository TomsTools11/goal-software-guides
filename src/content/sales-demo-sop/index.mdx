# GOAL Sales Demo SOP

Standardized process for running a complete, repeatable sales conversation from discovery through platform demo through close.

---

## Purpose & Context

<TipCallout variant="info" title="Purpose">
Standardize how GOAL team members run a complete, repeatable sales conversation from discovery through platform demo through close. The prospect should understand GOAL's value (CPA optimization + branded funnel + compliance + data feedback loop), see the platform in a consistent structure, and leave with a clear next step.
</TipCallout>

### When to Use This SOP

- First-time GOAL discovery + demo calls
- "Second look" follow-up demos that need a deeper walkthrough
- Internal coaching and scorecarding of recorded calls

### Roles

<InfoCard title="Rep / Demo Leader" icon="🎤">
Owns agenda, discovery, demo narrative, and next steps. This person drives the entire conversation and is responsible for the outcome.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Observer (Optional)" icon="📝">
Takes notes, timestamps key moments, captures objections, and scores the call using the scorecard in Appendix A. Observers do not speak unless invited.
</InfoCard>

### Key Definitions (Use Consistent Language)

<InfoCard title="Primary Optimization Metric" icon="💰">
**CPA (Cost per Acquisition)** — not CPL. Every conversation should anchor to acquisition cost, not lead cost.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Business Model" icon="🏗️">
**PPC (pay-per-click)**, not PPL (pay-per-lead). This distinction matters because it frames GOAL as a performance engine, not a lead vendor.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Funnel Goal" icon="🎯">
Reduce friction and increase high-intent submissions by using prefill and a localized branded experience. The consumer should feel like they are working with a local agent, not an aggregator.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Compliance Backbone ('Money Page')" icon="🛡️">
1:1 consent with redundant proof — TrustedForm + Draniya + confirmation layer. This is GOAL's litigation shield and a key differentiator.
</InfoCard>

<InfoCard title="Feedback Loop" icon="🔄">
Dispositions tie revenue outcomes back to source widgets/sites so GOAL can bid up/down by performance. This is the engine that makes optimization possible.
</InfoCard>

## 1) Opening & Framing (2–4 Minutes)

### Rapport + Two-Sided Agenda

Confirm time and desired outcome. Set a collaborative agenda: discovery first, then demo, then decide next steps.

<TipCallout variant="important" title="What Good Sounds Like">
"If it's okay, I'd like to understand your current lead flow and what success looks like, then I'll show you how GOAL works, and we'll decide the right next step by the end."
</TipCallout>

### Uncover the Catalyst ("Why Now?")

Ask what triggered interest today. Probe until you understand the real gap — burnout, lead quality swings, cost inflation, vendor opacity, or tech constraints.

---

## 2) Discovery & Qualification (8–15 Minutes)

<Accordion title="2.1 Early Qualification & Professional Disqualification">
Identify misfit fast (e.g., referral-only with no desire for digital growth). If misfit: offer to end early, respectfully. This builds trust and saves everyone's time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="2.2 Map the Current Lead Strategy">
Capture: lead sources (referrals vs paid), vendors and buying approach (data leads vs live transfers), team size and staffing realities.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="2.3 Infrastructure & Tech Stack Mapping">
Capture: CRM and constraints (e.g., Lead Manager), telephony setup and routing (IVR risk), any "bridge" requirements (e.g., Quote Burst intermediary into corporate systems).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="2.4 Quantify Financial Impact">
Try to get real numbers: monthly spend, CPA today (or proxy metrics), quote-to-close expectations. These numbers anchor the value conversation later.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="2.5 Big Picture Goals (6–18 Months)">
Growth goals, brand strategy, concerns about market shifts (rates, regs). Understanding the long game helps position GOAL as a strategic partner, not a tactical vendor.
</Accordion>

## 5) Tell the Full GOAL Story (Value Narrative Modules)

Use these modules to connect the demo back to the prospect's pain. You do not need to cover every module — pick the ones that map to the discovery findings.

<Accordion title="5.1 Consumer Journey + Branded Funnel Architecture">
Reduce drop-off by removing redundant data entry. Use prefill, Google Autocomplete, IP-based localization. Build trust with a branded "local authority" experience that differentiates from aggregator pages.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5.2 The 'Money Page' (Compliance + Litigation Protection)">
1:1 consent is non-negotiable. Redundant proof system: (1) TrustedForm for session replay, (2) Draniya for secondary validation, (3) Consent confirmation layer. Staff "standard response protocol": find the record quickly, send proof, end threats.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5.3 Exclusivity + Inbound Lead Flow ('Final Destination')">
Avoid lead resale dynamics. **KPI anchor:** "1 out of 10" rule — approximately 10% of prospects will initiate an inbound call. Inbound calls are "cold inbounds" and must ring directly to a person (no IVR).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5.4 Strategic Campaign Configuration + Bid Modifiers">
Use a lean filter strategy. Find "honey holes" by bidding up on valuable segments (based on real gaps). Geo strategy can shift to city-centric zips for tighter targeting.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5.5 The Data Feedback Loop (Dispositions → Source Optimization)">
Require raw disposition uploads. Tie outcomes to phone numbers and original prospects. Use it to: double down on high-performing sources, bid down or remove low-momentum sources, and reduce misattribution inside agencies.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5.6 AI Call Intelligence + Coaching Enablement">
QA at scale. Ask three questions on every call: (1) Did they quote it? (2) Did they sell it? (3) What are the next steps? Coach to mandates: cross-sell, scripting compliance, etc.
</Accordion>

## 6) Handle Common Questions

<Accordion title="How does GOAL learn?">
AI call intelligence + the data feedback loop. GOAL learns from every interaction by connecting lead data to real outcomes (quotes, sales, premiums). This creates a compounding optimization effect over time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why does lead quality vary?">
Source-side transparency and optimization. Not all traffic sources are equal. GOAL provides visibility into which publishers are driving quality and which are not — and the tools to act on it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What about volume vs quality?">
There are real tradeoffs. Bid modifiers, exclusions, and targeting let you dial in the balance. More filters = higher quality but lower volume. The right balance depends on the agency's capacity and goals.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is this CPL?">
No. Re-anchor to CPA. GOAL is a PPC (pay-per-click) model, not PPL (pay-per-lead). The difference is fundamental: you pay for clicks into a branded funnel you control, not for leads from a shared pool.
</Accordion>

## Appendix A: Sales Scorecard

Use this scorecard to evaluate every recorded call. Score each item as **Yes / Partial / No**.

<Accordion title="Phase 1: Opening & Framing">
1. **Rapport & agenda setting** — Did the rep confirm time, set a two-sided agenda, and establish a collaborative tone?
2. **Uncovering the catalyst ("Why now")** — Did the rep probe to understand the real trigger for the prospect's interest?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Phase 2: Initial Discovery & Strategic Qualification">
1. **Early qualification & disqualification potential** — Did the rep identify fit/misfit early and handle it professionally?
2. **Understanding high-level lead strategy** — Did the rep map current lead sources, vendors, and buying approach?
3. **Understanding current state (tenure & tech)** — Did the rep capture CRM, telephony, and integration details?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Phase 3: Deep Discovery & Problem Exploration">
1. **Uncovering operational problems** — Did the rep dig into what is broken and why?
2. **Quantifying financial impact & ROI** — Did the rep get real numbers (spend, CPA, volume)?
3. **Discovering business needs & long-term vision** — Did the rep understand 6–18 month goals?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Phase 4: Demo Execution">
1. **Connecting solution to problems** — Did the demo directly address the problems uncovered in discovery?
2. **Value proposition: agent branding** — Did the rep emphasize local branding and trust differentiation?
3. **Differentiation: PPC model vs PPL** — Did the rep clearly explain why GOAL is not a lead vendor?
4. **Demo: partnership & analytics (disposition engine)** — Did the rep show the feedback loop and optimization capabilities?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Phase 5: Conversational Skill">
1. **Active listening & curiosity** — Did the rep listen more than talk and ask follow-up questions?
2. **Dialogue control & pacing** — Did the rep manage time and keep the conversation on track?
3. **Strategic pausing & pulse checks** — Did the rep check in with the prospect and adjust based on reactions?
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Phase 6: Closing & Commitment">
1. **Pricing & expectation setting** — Did the rep present pricing clearly and set realistic timelines?
2. **Objection handling & concern resolution** — Did the rep address objections with data and empathy?
3. **Gaining commitment** — Did the rep ask for a clear next step?
4. **Outlining the path forward** — Did the rep provide a concrete plan for what happens next?
</Accordion>
